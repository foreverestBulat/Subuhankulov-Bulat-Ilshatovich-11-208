<p><b>Ask.com</b> (known originally as <b>Ask Jeeves</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Answer_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Answer engine">answer engine</a>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-auto3&#95;1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto3-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Online_magazine" title="Online magazine">e-magazine</a>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and former <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">web</a> <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" title="Search engine">search engine</a>, operated by Ask Media Group. It was conceptualized and developed in 1996 by <a href="/wiki/Garrett_Gruener" title="Garrett Gruener">Garrett Gruener</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Warthen" title="David Warthen">David Warthen</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Berkeley,_California" title="Berkeley, California">Berkeley</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.
</p><p>The original software was designed and implemented by <a href="/wiki/Gary_Chevsky" title="Gary Chevsky">Gary Chevsky</a>. Warthen, Chevsky and Justin Grant then led the <a href="/wiki/GUI" class="mw-redirect" title="GUI">GUI</a> development team, leading to the initial launch under the <a href="/wiki/Brand_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Brand name">brand name</a> of AskJeeves.com.
</p><p>In 2006, the "Jeeves" name was discontinued, and it became the Ask.com <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">web</a> <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" title="Search engine">search engine</a>, which had its own <a href="/wiki/Webcrawler" class="mw-redirect" title="Webcrawler">webcrawler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Algorithm" title="Algorithm">algorithm</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-:2&#95;3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In late 2010, faced with insurmountable competition from larger search engines, the company outsourced its <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">web</a> <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" title="Search engine">search</a> technology, and revived its function as a <a href="/wiki/Comparison_of_Q%26A_sites" title="Comparison of Q&amp;A sites">question and answer site</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Three venture capital companies, <a href="/wiki/Highland_Capital_Partners" title="Highland Capital Partners">Highland Capital Partners</a>, <a href="/wiki/Institutional_Venture_Partners" title="Institutional Venture Partners">Institutional Venture Partners</a>, and The RODA Group were early investors.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Ask.com is currently owned and operated by major U.S. media company <a href="/wiki/IAC_Inc." title="IAC Inc.">InterActiveCorp</a> (IAC), which acquired the Ask Media Group in 2005.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-:1&#95;6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-:3&#95;7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ask.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Jeeves-in-2009.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/c/cd/Jeeves-in-2009.jpg" decoding="async" width="127" height="269" class="mw-file-element" data-file-width="127" data-file-height="269" /></a><figcaption>3D render of Jeeves</figcaption></figure>
<p>Ask.com was originally known as Ask Jeeves,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-:0&#95;8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> "Jeeves" being the name of a "gentleman's personal gentleman", or <a href="/wiki/Valet" title="Valet">valet</a>, fetching answers to any question asked. The character was named after <a href="/wiki/Jeeves" title="Jeeves">Jeeves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bertie_Wooster" title="Bertie Wooster">Bertie Wooster</a>'s valet in the fictional works of <a href="/wiki/P._G._Wodehouse" title="P. G. Wodehouse">P.&#160;G. Wodehouse</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-auto3&#95;1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto3-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The original concept of Ask Jeeves was to allow users to get answers to questions in everyday, <a href="/wiki/Natural_language" title="Natural language">natural language</a>, and traditional keyword searching. Throughout its history, Ask was particularly well known for its answer functionality on the topics of mathematics, vocabulary, and unit conversion. Authors also contributed general articles about various other topics, similar to an <a href="/wiki/Encyclopedia" title="Encyclopedia">encyclopedia</a>. As other <a href="/wiki/InterActiveCorp" class="mw-redirect" title="InterActiveCorp">InterActiveCorp</a> publications were gradually connected with the Ask software, articles on thousands of topics became accessible.
</p><p>Ask Jeeves was launched as a beta version during mid-April 1997 and was initiated completely on June 1, 1997.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-:0&#95;8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>On September 18, 2001, Ask Jeeves acquired <a href="/wiki/Teoma" title="Teoma">Teoma</a> for more than $1.5 million.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-:22&#95;9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:22-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2005, Ask Jeeves was acquired by <a href="/wiki/IAC_Inc." title="IAC Inc.">IAC</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-:1&#95;6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-:3&#95;7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2006, the name "Jeeves" was dropped from Ask Jeeves, and the search engine renamed Ask.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-:2&#95;3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-:1&#95;6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>On May 16, 2006, Ask implemented a "Binoculars Site Preview" into its search results. On search results pages, the "binoculars" let searchers have a preview of the page they could visit with a mouse-over activating a pop-up screenshot.
</p><p>On June 5, 2007, Ask.com was redesigned with a 3D appearance.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2007, Ask released the AskEraser feature,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> allowing users to <a href="/wiki/Opt-out" title="Opt-out">opt-out</a> from tracking of <a href="/wiki/Web_search_query" class="mw-redirect" title="Web search query">search queries</a> and <a href="/wiki/IP_address" title="IP address">IP</a> and <a href="/wiki/HTTP_cookie" title="HTTP cookie">cookie</a> values. They also announced they would erase this data after 18 months if the AskEraser option was not set. <a href="/wiki/HTTP_cookies" class="mw-redirect" title="HTTP cookies">HTTP cookies</a> must be enabled for AskEraser to function.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ask_with_wikipedia.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/c/c6/Ask_with_wikipedia.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="397" class="mw-file-element" data-file-width="250" data-file-height="397" /></a><figcaption>An Ask.com search of Wikipedia, 2016</figcaption></figure>
<p>On July 4, 2008, Ask acquired Lexico Publishing Group, which owns <a href="/wiki/Dictionary.com" title="Dictionary.com">Dictionary.com</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thesaurus.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Thesaurus.com">Thesaurus.com</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Reference.com" title="Reference.com">Reference.com</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2008, Ask initiated the Ask Kids search engine designed for children.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-:3&#95;7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2009, for the UK version of Ask.com, Jeeves was redesigned as a <a href="/wiki/Computer-generated_imagery" title="Computer-generated imagery">CGI</a> character and the website was named once again Ask Jeeves, though international versions were still just Ask.com.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> His image remained on the UK website until July 21, 2016, though the Ask Jeeves name would continue to be used until September 21, 2016, when the website was renamed Ask.
</p><p>On July 26, 2010, Ask.com released a closed-beta Q&amp;A service. The service was released to the public on July 29, 2010.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ask.com initiated its mobile Q&amp;A application for the iPhone during late 2010.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Ask.com reached 100 million global users per month in 2012<sup id="cite&#95;ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> through its website with more than 2 million downloads of its flagship mobile app in that year.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The company has also released additional applications developed from its Q&amp;A experience, including Ask Around<sup id="cite&#95;ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> in 2011 and PollRoll<sup id="cite&#95;ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> in 2012.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Search_crawler_shut-down">Search crawler shut-down</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ask.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Search crawler shut-down"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In 2010, Ask.com shuttered its in-house web <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" title="Search engine">search engine</a> service, which was replaced by a new Ask search engine created by third-party developer.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-auto&#95;23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The restructuring programme included the termination of all development on the Ask.com <a href="/wiki/Webcrawler" class="mw-redirect" title="Webcrawler">webcrawler</a>, the outsourcing of most web search operations, and the loss of 130 search engineering jobs. The company cited market headwinds and intense competition from larger rivals, such as <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yahoo" title="Yahoo">Yahoo</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-auto&#95;23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Earlier in the year, Ask had initiated a Q&amp;A community for generating answers from real people as opposed to search algorithms. This new service was then combined with the existing question-and–answer repository, which included an extensive archive of query data. The new database and answer engine improved on the original capabilities of the AskJeeves Q&amp;A functions, generating many more answers.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Ask_Sponsored_Listings">Ask Sponsored Listings</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ask.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ask Sponsored Listings"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Ask Sponsored Listings, formerly the direct-sales division for Ask.com, is no longer available, having merged with Sendori, an operating business of IAC, in 2011.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Corporate_details">Corporate details</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ask.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Corporate details"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Askcomheadquarters.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Askcomheadquarters.jpg/250px-Askcomheadquarters.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="240" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Askcomheadquarters.jpg/500px-Askcomheadquarters.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="764" data-file-height="732" /></a><figcaption>Ask.com headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland</a>, California (photographed in 2006)</figcaption></figure>
<p>The Ask Media Group corporate headquarters is located in downtown <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>, based at the <a href="/wiki/555_City_Center" title="555 City Center">555 City Center</a> building within the <a href="/wiki/Oakland_City_Center" title="Oakland City Center">Oakland City Center</a> precinct.
</p><p>Ask Jeeves, Inc. stock traded on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ_stock_exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ stock exchange">NASDAQ stock exchange</a> from July 1999 to July 2005, using the ticker symbol ASKJ. In July 2005, the ASKJ ticker was retired upon the acquisition by IAC, valued at US$1.85&#160;billion.
</p><p>The current Ask Media Group president, <a href="/wiki/Doug_Leeds" title="Doug Leeds">Douglas Leeds</a> became CEO in 2010.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-IAC&#95;Management&#95;Bios&#95;26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IAC_Management_Bios-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Ask.com made two acquisitions as part of a larger strategy to offer more content on the Ask.com website. On July 2, 2012, Ask.com purchased content discovery start-up<sup id="cite&#95;ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> nRelate for an undisclosed amount. That was followed by the company's acquisition of expert advice and information site <a href="/wiki/About.com" class="mw-redirect" title="About.com">About.com</a>, which closed in September 2012.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>On August 14, 2014, Ask.com acquired popular social networking website <a href="/wiki/Ask.fm" title="Ask.fm">Ask.fm</a>, where users can ask other users questions, with the option of anonymity.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> As of August 14, 2014, Ask.fm had 180 million monthly unique users in more than 150 countries,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> with its largest user base in the United States.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Available on the web and as a mobile app, Ask.fm generates an estimated 20,000 questions per minute with approximately 45 percent of its mobile <a href="/wiki/Monthly_active_users" class="mw-redirect" title="Monthly active users">monthly active users</a> logging in daily.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Techcrunch&#95;32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Techcrunch-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> As of 2014<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ask.com&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, the mobile app has been downloaded more than 40 million times.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Techcrunch&#95;32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Techcrunch-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In 2021, Ask re-initiated its function <i>SymptomFind</i><sup id="cite&#95;ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and introduced the new finance-based site <i>Ask Money</i>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Marketing_and_promotion">Marketing and promotion</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ask.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Marketing and promotion"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>From November 1999, in some areas Ask Jeeves advertised on <a href="/wiki/Price_look-up_code#Promotion_via_PLU_stickers" title="Price look-up code">produce stickers</a> on apples, oranges and bananas. Questions such as "How many calories in a banana?" were printed alongside the Ask Jeeves web address.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-tampa-bay-times&#95;36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tampa-bay-times-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>A Jeeves balloon and a float appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s_Thanksgiving_Day_Parade" title="Macy&#39;s Thanksgiving Day Parade">Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade</a> during 2000–2004.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-tampa-bay-times&#95;36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tampa-bay-times-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Apostolos_Gerasoulis" title="Apostolos Gerasoulis">Apostolos Gerasoulis</a>, the co-creator of Ask's Teoma algorithmic search technology, featured in four television advertisements in 2007, extolling the virtues of Ask.com's usefulness for information relevance.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>After a hiatus from mass consumer marketing, Ask reinstated its website's format to emphasize questions and answers, and resumed advertising by television during the autumn of 2011.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Instead of national advertising, Ask emphasized local markets. During the summer of 2012, initiated a national cinema campaign,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> along with other out-of-home tactics in certain markets such as New York and <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>As part of a Seattle-based local market effort, Ask.com initiated its campaign "You Asked We Answered"<sup id="cite&#95;ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> during 2012, in which the company "answered" residents' main complaints about living in their city, including easing morning commutes and stadium traffic, as well as keeping the local Parks and Recreation department's wading pools open.
</p><p>On January 14, 2009, Ask.com became the official sponsor of 2000 <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a> <a href="/wiki/Sprint_Cup_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="Sprint Cup Series">Sprint Cup Series</a> Champion <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Labonte" title="Bobby Labonte">Bobby Labonte</a>'s No.&#160;96 <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. Ask would become the official search engine of NASCAR.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ask.com was the primary sponsor for the No.&#160;96 for 18 of the first 21 races and had rights to increase this to a total of 29 races that season.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Ask.com car debuted in the 2009 Bud Shootout where it failed to finish the race, but subsequently returned strongly, placing as high as 5th in a March 1, 2009, <a href="/wiki/2009_Shelby_427" title="2009 Shelby 427">Shelby 427</a> race at <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Motor_Speedway" title="Las Vegas Motor Speedway">Las Vegas Motor Speedway</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ask.com's foray into NASCAR represented the first instance of its venture into what it terms "<a href="/wiki/Vertical_search" title="Vertical search">Super Verticals</a>".<sup id="cite&#95;ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
