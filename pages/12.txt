<p><b>Panagiotis G. Ipeirotis</b> (also known as <b>Panos Ipeirotis</b>; born May 3, 1976, in <a href="/wiki/Serres,_Greece" class="mw-redirect" title="Serres, Greece">Serres, Greece</a>) is a Greek-American <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> and the Merchants' Council Professor of Technology and Business at the <a href="/wiki/New_York_University_Stern_School_of_Business" title="New York University Stern School of Business">New York University Stern School of Business</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-SternBio&#95;5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SternBio-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-TOPSFaculty&#95;6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TOPSFaculty-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> His research focuses on <a href="/wiki/Data_mining" title="Data mining">data mining</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crowdsourcing" title="Crowdsourcing">crowdsourcing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Human-based_computation" title="Human-based computation">human computation</a>, and the economics of online information systems.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-EconoMining&#95;7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EconoMining-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Ipeirotis received the <a href="/wiki/Lagrange_Prize" title="Lagrange Prize">Lagrange Prize</a> in Complex Systems (2015)<sup id="cite&#95;ref-LagrangePrize&#95;1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LagrangePrize-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/ACM_SIGKDD" class="mw-redirect" title="ACM SIGKDD">ACM SIGKDD</a> Test of Time Award (2020).<sup id="cite&#95;ref-KDDAward&#95;2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KDDAward-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> His research on <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Mechanical_Turk" title="Amazon Mechanical Turk">Amazon Mechanical Turk</a> and crowdsourcing quality management has been discussed in publications including <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Businessweek" title="Bloomberg Businessweek">Bloomberg Businessweek</a></i>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-NYTFakeReviews&#95;8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTFakeReviews-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-EconomistMTurk&#95;9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EconomistMTurk-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-Bloomberg2011&#95;10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg2011-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to his academic career, Ipeirotis co-founded the AI consulting firm Detectica in 2015, which was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Compass,_Inc." title="Compass, Inc.">Compass, Inc.</a> in 2019.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-WSJDetecticaAcquisition&#95;11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJDetecticaAcquisition-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He has also held research positions at <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> (2013–2014), where he conducted research on crowdsourcing methods to improve the <a href="/wiki/Google_Knowledge_Graph" class="mw-redirect" title="Google Knowledge Graph">Google Knowledge Graph</a>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-GoogleQuizz&#95;12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoogleQuizz-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-GoogleTrust&#95;13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoogleTrust-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and at <a href="/wiki/Meta_Platforms" title="Meta Platforms">Meta</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Reality_Labs" title="Reality Labs">Reality Labs</a> division (2024–2025), where he worked on machine learning deployment for wearable devices.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-SternBio&#95;5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SternBio-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-MITCCI&#95;14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MITCCI-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ipeirotis also authors the blog "A Computer Scientist in a Business School," which has been cited in media coverage.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-BlogBehindEnemyLines&#95;15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BlogBehindEnemyLines-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-SlateReviews&#95;16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SlateReviews-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Early_life">Early life</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Ipeirotis was born on May 3, 1976, in <a href="/wiki/Serres" title="Serres">Serres</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-SerresNews&#95;4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SerresNews-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1994, he won a Gold Medal at the 8th National Greek Competition in Chemistry.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-GreekChemComp&#95;17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GreekChemComp-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Later that year, he represented Greece at the <a href="/wiki/International_Chemistry_Olympiad" title="International Chemistry Olympiad">26th International Chemistry Olympiad</a> in Oslo, Norway.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-IChO1994&#95;18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IChO1994-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Education">Education</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Ipeirotis earned his Diploma in Computer Engineering and Informatics (CEID) from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Patras" title="University of Patras">University of Patras</a> in 1999.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-SternBio&#95;5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SternBio-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He pursued graduate studies at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> under the supervision of Luis Gravano, receiving his <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">M.Sc.</a> in 2001, <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Philosophy" title="Master of Philosophy">M.Phil.</a> in 2003, and <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph.D.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Computer_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer Science">Computer Science</a> in 2004.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-SternBio&#95;5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SternBio-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Academic_career">Academic career</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Academic career"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Ipeirotis began his academic career as a graduate research assistant at Columbia University (1999–2004). In 2004, he joined the Department of Information, Operations, and Management Sciences at the <a href="/wiki/New_York_University_Stern_School_of_Business" title="New York University Stern School of Business">New York University Stern School of Business</a> as an Assistant Professor. He was promoted to Associate Professor in 2010 and Full Professor in 2016. He holds a courtesy appointment at the <a href="/wiki/Courant_Institute_of_Mathematical_Sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences">Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences</a><sup id="cite&#95;ref-CourantFaculty&#95;19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CourantFaculty-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and is an associated faculty member at the <a href="/wiki/NYU_Center_for_Data_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="NYU Center for Data Science">NYU Center for Data Science</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-CDSAssociated&#95;20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CDSAssociated-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Ipeirotis has served as Program Co-Chair for the 13th ACM Conference on Electronic Commerce in 2012,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-ACMEC2012&#95;21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACMEC2012-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> as General Co-Chair of the AAAI Conference on Human Computation and Crowdsourcing (HCOMP) in 2015,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-HCOMP2015&#95;22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HCOMP2015-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and as Technical Program Co-Chair for <a href="/wiki/The_Web_Conference" title="The Web Conference">The Web Conference</a> 2018 (WWW 2018).<sup id="cite&#95;ref-WWW2018&#95;23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WWW2018-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He has served on the editorial boards of <i><a href="/wiki/Management_Science_(journal)" title="Management Science (journal)">Management Science</a></i> and <i>IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering</i>, and was a founding co-Editor-in-Chief of the journal <i>Collective Intelligence</i> launched in 2022 as a collaboration between <a href="/wiki/SAGE_Publications" class="mw-redirect" title="SAGE Publications">SAGE Publications</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nesta_(charity)" title="Nesta (charity)">Nesta</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-CollectiveIntelligence&#95;24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CollectiveIntelligence-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-ACMCollectiveIntelligence&#95;25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACMCollectiveIntelligence-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Academic_lineage_and_doctoral_students">Academic lineage and doctoral students</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Academic lineage and doctoral students"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Ipeirotis completed his PhD at Columbia University under the supervision of Luis Gravano, himself a student of <a href="/wiki/Hector_Garcia-Molina" class="mw-redirect" title="Hector Garcia-Molina">Hector Garcia-Molina</a> at Stanford. According to the <a href="/wiki/Mathematics_Genealogy_Project" title="Mathematics Genealogy Project">Mathematics Genealogy Project</a>, Ipeirotis has advised seven doctoral students at NYU and Columbia.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-MathGenealogy&#95;35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MathGenealogy-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>His former students include Beibei Li, now a professor at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">Carnegie Mellon University</a>'s Heinz College and recipient of the 2019 INFORMS ISS Sandra A. Slaughter Early Career Award,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-SlaughterAward&#95;36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SlaughterAward-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and Marios Kokkodis, recipient of the 2020 INFORMS ISS Gordon B. Davis Young Scholar Award.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-DavisAward&#95;37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DavisAward-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Former postdoctoral researcher Djellel Difallah is now Assistant Professor and Program Head at <a href="/wiki/New_York_University_Abu_Dhabi" title="New York University Abu Dhabi">New York University Abu Dhabi</a> and previously served as a Research Scientist at the <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Wikimedia Foundation">Wikimedia Foundation</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-DifallahNYUAD&#95;38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DifallahNYUAD-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Research">Research</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Research"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Ipeirotis's work explores the intersection of computer science and economics, an approach he and collaborators have termed "EconoMining." His research has been published in venues such as <i><a href="/wiki/Management_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Management Science">Management Science</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Information_Systems_Research" title="Information Systems Research">Information Systems Research</a></i>, and <i>IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering</i>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-SternBio&#95;5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SternBio-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Crowdsourcing_and_human_computation">Crowdsourcing and human computation</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Crowdsourcing and human computation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Ipeirotis has published research on <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Mechanical_Turk" title="Amazon Mechanical Turk">Amazon Mechanical Turk</a> (MTurk). His 2010 paper "Running Experiments on Amazon Mechanical Turk,"<sup id="cite&#95;ref-MTurkPaper&#95;39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTurkPaper-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> co-authored with Gabriele Paolacci and Jesse Chandler, provided methodological guidance for using crowdsourcing platforms in behavioral research.
</p><p>In 2010, his studies revealing that approximately 40% of tasks posted by new requesters on MTurk involved spam or low-quality content received media coverage.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-TIMEMTurk&#95;30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TIMEMTurk-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-TechReview&#95;29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TechReview-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-BusinessInsider&#95;32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BusinessInsider-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-WashPost&#95;31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WashPost-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> His 2010 demographic analysis of the MTurk workforce provided early data on worker motivations, geographic distribution, and income levels, and has been cited by researchers studying crowdsourcing platforms.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-MTurkDemographics&#95;40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTurkDemographics-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-FortCoalMine&#95;41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FortCoalMine-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2018 and 2019, media outlets continued to cite Ipeirotis's research on MTurk data quality as concerns about automated responses on the platform grew.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-WiredBotPanic&#95;42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WiredBotPanic-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-NYTMTurk2019&#95;26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTMTurk2019-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This line of work led to the development of quality management techniques for crowdsourcing, including the "Get Another Label" framework for improving data quality using multiple noisy labelers, which received the SIGKDD Test of Time Award in 2020.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-KDDAward&#95;2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KDDAward-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>His research on crowdsourcing best practices also influenced journalism; <a href="/wiki/ProPublica" title="ProPublica">ProPublica</a> published a "Guide to Mechanical Turk" for investigative reporters that drew on Ipeirotis's research and blog posts, citing his work on data quality through multiple worker verification.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-ProPublicaGuide&#95;43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ProPublicaGuide-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>To support this research, Ipeirotis developed "MTurk Tracker," a set of tracking tools for the MTurk platform. The first tool tracked workforce demographics using capture-recapture techniques to estimate the size and composition of the workforce in near real-time. The second tool periodically downloaded all tasks posted on the platform, enabling analysis of the crowdsourcing marketplace's size and activity; Amazon eventually blocked this tracker. The <a href="/wiki/Pew_Research_Center" title="Pew Research Center">Pew Research Center</a>'s 2016 report "Research in the Crowdsourcing Age" drew on data from the task tracker, explicitly crediting "<i>the online tool mturk-tracker, which is run by Dr. Panagiotis G. Ipeirotis.</i>"<sup id="cite&#95;ref-PewMTurkTracker&#95;44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PewMTurkTracker-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-PewMTurkSize&#95;45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PewMTurkSize-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>During a 2013–2014 sabbatical at Google, Ipeirotis co-developed "Quizz," a gamified crowdsourcing system for improving the <a href="/wiki/Google_Knowledge_Graph" class="mw-redirect" title="Google Knowledge Graph">Google Knowledge Graph</a>. The system presented data collection as trivia quizzes targeted by domain expertise, using calibration questions with known answers to assess user reliability in real-time.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-GoogleQuizz&#95;12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoogleQuizz-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-SlawskiQuizz&#95;46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SlawskiQuizz-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Data_quality_and_record_linkage">Data quality and record linkage</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Data quality and record linkage"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Ipeirotis has published research on duplicate record detection and data quality. His 2007 survey "Duplicate Record Detection: A Survey" in <i>IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering</i>, co-authored with Ahmed Elmagarmid and Vassilios Verykios, has been cited in the database research community.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-DuplicateSurvey&#95;47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DuplicateSurvey-47"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>47<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Online_reputation_and_user-generated_content">Online reputation and user-generated content</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Online reputation and user-generated content"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>His collaborative work with <a href="/wiki/Anindya_Ghose" title="Anindya Ghose">Anindya Ghose</a> on the economic value of textual content in product reviews quantified the pricing power derived from user-generated content.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-PricingPower&#95;48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PricingPower-48"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>48<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A related 2011 study demonstrated that the quality of spelling and grammar in product reviews significantly affects perceived helpfulness and product sales.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-ReviewHelpfulness&#95;49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReviewHelpfulness-49"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>49<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This finding was discussed in <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harvard_Business_Review" title="Harvard Business Review">Harvard Business Review</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Slate_(magazine)" title="Slate (magazine)">Slate</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Freakonomics" title="Freakonomics">Freakonomics</a></i>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>50<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>51<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>52<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>53<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Applied_research_and_industry">Applied research and industry</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Applied research and industry"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Early_industry_work_(2009–2014)"><span id="Early_industry_work_.282009.E2.80.932014.29"></span>Early industry work (2009–2014)</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Early industry work (2009–2014)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Beginning in 2009, Ipeirotis served as an early advisor to <a href="/wiki/Integral_Ad_Science" title="Integral Ad Science">Integral Ad Science</a> (originally AdSafe Media), contributing to the development of machine learning systems for detecting inappropriate web content and advertising fraud.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-DetecticaAbout&#95;54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DetecticaAbout-54"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>54<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2011, working with AdSafe engineers, he uncovered a <a href="/wiki/Click_fraud" title="Click fraud">click fraud</a> scheme that used hidden <a href="/wiki/HTML_element#Frames" title="HTML element">iframes</a> to generate fraudulent ad impressions, which he termed "traffic laundering."<sup id="cite&#95;ref-MITTechReview2011&#95;55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MITTechReview2011-55"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>55<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The investigation, which estimated the scheme generated hundreds of thousands of dollars monthly, was reported by <i>The Wall Street Journal</i> and <i>MIT Technology Review</i> and led to an <a href="/wiki/FBI" class="mw-redirect" title="FBI">FBI</a> referral.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-WSJ2011AdFraud&#95;27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ2011AdFraud-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-MITTechReview2011&#95;55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MITTechReview2011-55"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>55<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Ipeirotis co-founded <a href="/wiki/Tagasauris" title="Tagasauris">Tagasauris</a>, a media annotation startup where he served as Chief Scientist until 2013.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Bloomberg2013&#95;33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg2013-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The company combined crowdsourcing with semantic graph technology to tag photo archives. A notable accomplishment was the discovery of approximately two dozen "lost" photographs from the 1973 filming of "<a href="/wiki/American_Graffiti" title="American Graffiti">American Graffiti</a>" in the <a href="/wiki/Magnum_Photos" title="Magnum Photos">Magnum Photos</a> archive; the system identified the photos by using crowdworkers to tag individuals and machine learning to connect them via shared film appearances.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-WiredGraffiti&#95;56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WiredGraffiti-56"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>56<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-MITTechReviewGraffiti&#95;57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MITTechReviewGraffiti-57"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>57<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>He also served as Academic-in-Residence at <a href="/wiki/Upwork" title="Upwork">Upwork</a> (then oDesk) in 2012 and as a Visiting Scientist at <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> from 2013 to 2014.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-SternBio&#95;5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SternBio-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Policy_research_and_World_Bank">Policy research and World Bank</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Policy research and World Bank"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In 2015, Ipeirotis collaborated with the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" class="mw-redirect" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> on a report titled "The Global Opportunity in Online Outsourcing," which examined the potential of digital labor markets to provide employment pathways in developing nations.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-WorldBankReport&#95;58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WorldBankReport-58"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>58<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Drawing on his empirical research from Mechanical Turk and oDesk, the report estimated the online outsourcing market would grow to $15–25 billion by 2020 and identified barriers preventing workers in the <a href="/wiki/Global_South" class="mw-redirect" title="Global South">Global South</a> from accessing these jobs, including lack of reliable internet infrastructure and the "reputation cold start" problem. The report has been cited in subsequent policy frameworks by the <a href="/wiki/International_Labour_Organization" title="International Labour Organization">International Labour Organization</a> and the <a href="/wiki/RAND_Corporation" title="RAND Corporation">RAND Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-RANDOnlineOutsourcing&#95;59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RANDOnlineOutsourcing-59"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>59<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Detectica_and_Compass_(2015–2022)"><span id="Detectica_and_Compass_.282015.E2.80.932022.29"></span>Detectica and Compass (2015–2022)</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Detectica and Compass (2015–2022)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In 2015, Ipeirotis co-founded Detectica with <a href="/wiki/Foster_Provost" title="Foster Provost">Foster Provost</a> and Josh Attenberg, offering AI strategy consulting and machine learning solutions for business applications.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Techweek&#95;60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Techweek-60"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>60<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The company developed AI-driven compliance monitoring systems for financial institutions. Prior to founding Detectica, the team had designed and built the founding data science architecture for Integral Ad Science.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Techweek&#95;60-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Techweek-60"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>60<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Detectica was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Compass,_Inc." title="Compass, Inc.">Compass, Inc.</a> in November 2019.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-WSJDetecticaAcquisition&#95;11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJDetecticaAcquisition-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> At Compass, the Detectica team developed "Likely to Sell," a predictive analytics system that identifies properties likely to enter the market; the company cited the tool as a significant revenue contributor in earnings calls.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-CompassQ42021&#95;61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CompassQ42021-61"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>61<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-CompassQ12023&#95;62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CompassQ12023-62"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>62<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Public_engagement_and_pedagogy">Public engagement and pedagogy</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Public engagement and pedagogy"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Academic_integrity_debates">Academic integrity debates</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Academic integrity debates"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In July 2011, Ipeirotis published a blog post titled "Why I will never pursue cheating again," describing his experience using <a href="/wiki/Turnitin" title="Turnitin">Turnitin</a> software to detect that about 20 percent of the students in his roughly 100-person "Information Technology in Business and Society" course had plagiarized.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-InsideHigherEd2011&#95;63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-InsideHigherEd2011-63"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>63<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The post criticized the administrative burden placed on faculty who enforce academic integrity policies and went viral.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Chronicle2011&#95;64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chronicle2011-64"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>64<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ipeirotis temporarily removed the post after a complaint alleged potential violations of the <a href="/wiki/Family_Educational_Rights_and_Privacy_Act" title="Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act">Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act</a> (FERPA) due to included student communications.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Chronicle2011&#95;64-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chronicle2011-64"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>64<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <i>Business Insider</i> reported that Ipeirotis said his student evaluations declined after the plagiarism cases, costing him part of his annual raise;<sup id="cite&#95;ref-BusinessInsiderCheating&#95;65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BusinessInsiderCheating-65"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>65<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> NYU Stern vice dean Ingo Walter responded that faculty are not penalized for enforcing academic integrity and that student evaluations from such cases are excluded from review.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-InsideHigherEd2011&#95;63-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-InsideHigherEd2011-63"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>63<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The incident prompted discussion about academic integrity enforcement, faculty incentives, and student privacy.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-InsideHigherEd2011&#95;63-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-InsideHigherEd2011-63"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>63<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-Chronicle2011&#95;64-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chronicle2011-64"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>64<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The case was subsequently cited in <i><a href="/wiki/Nature_(journal)" title="Nature (journal)">Nature</a></i> in a 2012 commentary on plagiarism detection in academia.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-NaturePlagiarism&#95;66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NaturePlagiarism-66"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>66<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Security_vulnerability_discovery">Security vulnerability discovery</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Security vulnerability discovery"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In April 2012, Ipeirotis accidentally discovered a vulnerability in Google Spreadsheets that he termed a "Denial of Money" attack. While running a crowdsourcing experiment that required image classification, he uploaded 25,000 image URLs (hosted on his Amazon S3 bucket) into a Google Spreadsheet. Google's Feedfetcher bot aggressively re-downloaded all images from his S3 bucket every time the spreadsheet was processed, generating massive outgoing bandwidth. Ipeirotis reported receiving an <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services" title="Amazon Web Services">Amazon Web Services</a> bill of $1,177.76—approximately ten times his normal monthly rate.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-WiredDoM&#95;67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WiredDoM-67"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>67<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The incident highlighted a novel attack vector: a malicious actor could create a Google Sheet with thousands of links to a victim's large files, causing Google's servers to repeatedly request the files, resulting in either financial damage or a <a href="/wiki/Denial-of-service_attack" title="Denial-of-service attack">denial of service</a>. The discovery was covered in technology publications including <i><a href="/wiki/Wired_(magazine)" title="Wired (magazine)">Wired</a></i> and <i>The Economist</i>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-EconomistDoM&#95;68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EconomistDoM-68"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>68<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-WiredDoM&#95;67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WiredDoM-67"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>67<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-BIDoM&#95;69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BIDoM-69"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>69<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-HackerNewsDoM&#95;70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HackerNewsDoM-70"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>70<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Amazon subsequently forgave the charges, and Google investigated the behavior.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-WiredDoM&#95;67-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WiredDoM-67"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>67<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="AI-powered_assessment">AI-powered assessment</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: AI-powered assessment"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In December 2025, Ipeirotis developed an AI-driven oral examination system using <a href="/wiki/ElevenLabs" title="ElevenLabs">ElevenLabs</a> voice agents to address concerns about students using generative AI tools to submit assignments while lacking deeper understanding. The system, which cost approximately $0.42 per student to administer, used a voice AI agent to call students and ask personalized questions about their submitted work.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-BusinessInsider2026&#95;71-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BusinessInsider2026-71"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>71<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-TheDecoder2026&#95;72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheDecoder2026-72"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>72<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Awards_and_honors">Awards and honors</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panos_Ipeirotis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Awards and honors"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<ul><li><b>2020:</b> <a href="/wiki/ACM_SIGKDD" class="mw-redirect" title="ACM SIGKDD">ACM SIGKDD</a> Test of Time Award for Research (with Foster Provost and Victor S. Sheng). The Test of Time Award honors papers from approximately ten years prior that have had lasting impact on the field of knowledge discovery and data mining. The award recognized their 2008 paper "Get Another Label?
