<p><b>Yahoo Search</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" title="Search engine">search engine</a> owned and operated by <a href="/wiki/Yahoo" title="Yahoo">Yahoo</a>, using <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Bing" title="Microsoft Bing">Microsoft Bing</a> to generate results.
</p><p>When first released in 1994, Yahoo Search sent <a href="/wiki/Web_search_query" class="mw-redirect" title="Web search query">queries</a> to a searchable index of pages supplemented with its directory of <a href="/wiki/Website" title="Website">websites</a>. <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">Web crawling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Data_warehouse" title="Data warehouse">data housing</a> was executed by <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> from 2000 until 2004, when Yahoo created its own crawler.
</p><p>In July 2009, Yahoo and <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> made a deal in which Bing would generate Yahoo Search results.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Microsoft&#95;and&#95;Yahoo&#95;seal&#95;web&#95;deal&#95;2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Microsoft_and_Yahoo_seal_web_deal-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Google provided some results from 2015 to 2018.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-tc-changes&#95;3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tc-changes-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (October 2025)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>As of August 2025, Yahoo had a search <a href="/wiki/Market_share" title="Market share">market share</a> in the U.S. of 3.22 percent.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahoo_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Development_of_search_technology">Development of search technology</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahoo_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development of search technology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Yahoo Search began as a tool to search <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Directory" class="mw-redirect" title="Yahoo! Directory">Yahoo! Directory</a>, which was launched in 1994 by <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Yang" title="Jerry Yang">Jerry Yang</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Filo" title="David Filo">David Filo</a>, then students at <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It became the first popular search engine on the Web,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> despite being manually curated, unlike its competitors.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2000, Yahoo Search began licensing results from <a href="/wiki/Google_Search" title="Google Search">Google Search</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Seeking to provide its own search engine results, in December 2002 Yahoo announced it would acquire search engine company <a href="/wiki/Inktomi_(company)" class="mw-redirect" title="Inktomi (company)">Inktomi</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The acquisition completed in March 2003.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Grossman&#95;10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grossman-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ysearch_2005.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/77/Ysearch_2005.png/250px-Ysearch_2005.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="214" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/7/77/Ysearch_2005.png 1.5x" data-file-width="350" data-file-height="300" /></a><figcaption>Yahoo! Search page in 2005</figcaption></figure>
<p>In October 2003, Yahoo purchased <a href="/wiki/Overture_Services,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Overture Services, Inc.">Overture Services, Inc.</a>, which included the <a href="/wiki/AlltheWeb" title="AlltheWeb">AlltheWeb</a> and <a href="/wiki/AltaVista" title="AltaVista">AltaVista</a> search engines.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Grossman&#95;10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grossman-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In February 2004, Yahoo Search replaced Google's results with results from its own <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">web crawler</a>, Yahoo Search Technology. Yahoo Search Technology combined the capabilities of search engine companies Yahoo had acquired.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The next month, the site began practicing <a href="/wiki/Paid_inclusion" title="Paid inclusion">paid inclusion</a> to guarantee listing in search; however, most results continued to come from free web crawling.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, Yahoo Search results began including links to previous versions of pages archived on the <a href="/wiki/Wayback_Machine" title="Wayback Machine">Wayback Machine</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Evolution_of_search_features">Evolution of search features</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahoo_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Evolution of search features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Yahoo released Panama, its updated sponsored search platform, in 2007.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The platform introduced ad matching based on a mix of bid prices, ad quality, and relevance to the user.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Also in 2007, Yahoo released OneSearch, its Internet search system for mobile phones.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Yahoo used <a href="/wiki/Novarra" class="mw-redirect" title="Novarra">Novarra</a>'s mobile content transcoding service for OneSearch.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2007, Yahoo Search added Search Assist, which provided real-time query suggestions and related concepts as queries were typed.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The site also added search for audio, video, and photos.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Yahoo began piloting Search Shortcuts, which highlighted key information like ratings and reviews and official sites in search results.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Yahoo Shortcuts, a separate tool, launched in December 2007 as a plugin to add contextual links and freely licensed <a href="/wiki/Flickr" title="Flickr">Flickr</a> photos to content on <a href="/wiki/WordPress" title="WordPress">WordPress</a> sites quickly.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:SearchScan.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/24/SearchScan.png/330px-SearchScan.png" decoding="async" width="300" height="127" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/2/24/SearchScan.png 1.5x" data-file-width="485" data-file-height="205" /></a><figcaption>SearchScan in action</figcaption></figure><p> In May 2008, Yahoo introduced SearchScan, a feature to alert search users of <a href="/wiki/Computer_virus" title="Computer virus">viruses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spyware" title="Spyware">spyware</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spam_(electronic)" class="mw-redirect" title="Spam (electronic)">spam</a> websites,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> with support from <a href="/wiki/McAfee_WebAdvisor" title="McAfee WebAdvisor">McAfee SiteAdvisor</a> technology.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> That same month, Yahoo piloted a new search interface called Yahoo Glue, displaying different kinds of search results (e.g., photos and videos, in addition to hyperlinks) together on the same page.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Yahoo Glue expanded to the US that November.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ysearch_2008.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/10/Ysearch_2008.png/250px-Ysearch_2008.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="66" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/10/Ysearch_2008.png/500px-Ysearch_2008.png 1.5x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="159" /></a><figcaption>Yahoo! Search page in 2008</figcaption></figure>
<p>In July 2008, Yahoo Search introduced <a href="/wiki/Yahoo_Search_BOSS" title="Yahoo Search BOSS">Yahoo Search BOSS</a> ("Build your Own Search Service"). This service allows developers to use Yahoo's system for indexing information and images and create custom search engines.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Yahoo introduced Search Direct, which suggests results as the user types, in March 2011.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The function aims to answer user questions without loading a new page.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2012, the company released <a href="/wiki/Yahoo_Axis" title="Yahoo Axis">Yahoo Axis</a>, a iOS app and desktop <a href="/wiki/Browser_extension" title="Browser extension">browser extension</a> to provide a streamlined search experience.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Axis displayed search results as visual snapshots in a horizontal carousel, allowing users to preview pages without leaving the results page.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was sunset in June 2013.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Yahoo published Aviate, its search application for <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> devices, in June 2014.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The app was updated with browser-less search six months later.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Partnerships">Partnerships</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahoo_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Partnerships"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In July 2009, Yahoo and Microsoft announced a deal in which <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bing (search engine)">Bing</a> would provide results for Yahoo Search. In exchange, the deal also established a "search alliance" between Yahoo and Bing to sell advertising on both services,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Pian&#95;Chan&#95;35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pian_Chan-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> with Yahoo receiving 88% of search ad sales revenue on its site for the first five years, as well as the right to sell advertisements on certain Microsoft sites.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Microsoft&#95;and&#95;Yahoo&#95;seal&#95;web&#95;deal&#95;2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Microsoft_and_Yahoo_seal_web_deal-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The search alliance was implemented in 2010,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Pian&#95;Chan&#95;35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pian_Chan-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and all Yahoo-sponsored ad clients transitioned to <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Advertising" title="Microsoft Advertising">Microsoft adCenter</a> by the end of the year.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ysearch_2011.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bc/Ysearch_2011.png/250px-Ysearch_2011.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="120" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/bc/Ysearch_2011.png 1.5x" data-file-width="469" data-file-height="225" /></a><figcaption>Yahoo! Search page in 2011</figcaption></figure><p> In March 2014, Yahoo partnered with <a href="/wiki/Yelp" title="Yelp">Yelp</a> to integrate its reviews and user-contributed photos into Yahoo Search.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-tc-yelppartnership&#95;37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tc-yelppartnership-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In November 2014, Mozilla signed a five-year partnership with Yahoo, making Yahoo Search the default search engine for <a href="/wiki/Firefox" title="Firefox">Firefox</a> browsers in the US.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Yahoo and Microsoft modified their partnership in April 2015 to require Bing results only on the "majority" of desktop traffic, allowing Yahoo to enter into non-exclusive deals for search on mobile platforms and the remainder of desktop traffic.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-ars-april2015deal&#95;39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ars-april2015deal-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-tc-changes&#95;3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tc-changes-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The amendment also gave either company the ability to terminate the contract with four months' notice.  In October 2015, Yahoo reached an agreement with Google to provide services to Yahoo Search through the end of 2018, including advertising, search, and image search.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-verge-googleyahoo&#95;40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-verge-googleyahoo-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2020, Yahoo's parent Verizon launched OneSearch, a privacy-focused <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" title="Search engine">search engine</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> OneSearch uses Bing search results.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-lifehacker/1841042875&#95;42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lifehacker/1841042875-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The service selects ads based on keywords rather than <a href="/wiki/HTTP_cookies" class="mw-redirect" title="HTTP cookies">HTTP cookies</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
