<p><b>Marc-Alexander Najork</b> is a distinguished research scientist at <a href="/wiki/Google_DeepMind" title="Google DeepMind">Google DeepMind</a> working on <a href="/wiki/Generative_artificial_intelligence" title="Generative artificial intelligence">generative artificial intelligence</a>. His career spans research at <a href="/wiki/Google_Research" title="Google Research">Google Research</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Research" title="Microsoft Research">Microsoft Research</a> and <a href="/wiki/DEC_Systems_Research_Center" title="DEC Systems Research Center">DEC Systems Research Center</a> on data retrieval, link-based ranking algorithms, detection of spam websites, a distributed web crawler, and a scripting system for 3D animations, among others. He has served in several leadership positions for publications and conferences of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a>. He has coauthored over a hundred research papers and holds over 40 US patents.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He is an <a href="/wiki/IEEE_Fellow" class="mw-redirect" title="IEEE Fellow">IEEE Fellow</a>, <a href="/wiki/ACM_Fellow" title="ACM Fellow">ACM Fellow</a>, <a href="/wiki/AAAS_Fellow" class="mw-redirect" title="AAAS Fellow">AAAS Fellow</a>, and AAIA Fellow.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Education">Education</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Najork&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Marc graduated with a <a href="/wiki/Diplom" title="Diplom">diplom</a> in <a href="/wiki/Business_informatics" title="Business informatics">wirtschaftsinformatik</a> from <a href="/wiki/Technische_Universit%C3%A4t_Darmstadt" title="Technische Universität Darmstadt">Technische Universität Darmstadt</a> in 1989. He completed his Ph.D. in computer science at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Illinois_Urbana-Champaign" title="University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign">University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign</a>. Overseen by Simon M. Kaplan, his thesis was on Cube, "the <a href="/wiki/Visual_programming_language" title="Visual programming language">first visual language</a> to employ a three-dimensional syntax."<sup id="cite&#95;ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The programming language was also supported by a grant from the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> 
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Career">Career</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Najork&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In 1993, Marc joined <a href="/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation" title="Digital Equipment Corporation">Digital Equipment Corporation</a> which was later merged with <a href="/wiki/Compaq" title="Compaq">Compaq</a>. One of his contributions was to Mercator, an extendable, Java-based, high-performance web crawler which was integrated into <a href="/wiki/AltaVista" title="AltaVista">AltaVista</a> in 2001.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Marc Najork and Marc Brown were the main contributors to JCAT, a Java-based algorithm animation system with classroom applications.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Luca_Cardelli" title="Luca Cardelli">Luca Cardelli</a> developed <a href="/wiki/Obliq" title="Obliq">Obliq</a>, which Marc used to make Obliq-3D, a fast-turnaround 3D animation system.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In 2001, he joined <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Research_Silicon_Valley" class="mw-redirect" title="Microsoft Research Silicon Valley">Microsoft Research Silicon Valley</a>. He contributed to Boxwood, a distributed, scalable <a href="/wiki/B-tree" title="B-tree">B-tree</a> which serves as a foundation for storage infrastructure.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The creation of PageTurner, with Dennis Fetterly and Mark Manasse, was designed to study the evolution of webpages. They also found that past behavior of changes in websites was indicative of future changes on that site. Further study of the data showed that statistical anomalies were a good predictor of spam, which led Microsoft to file a patent later used by <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Bing" title="Microsoft Bing">Microsoft Bing</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Marc also developed the Scalable Hyperlink Store, which is a scalable, fault-tolerant, and incrementally updateable database for storing large portions of a <a href="/wiki/Webgraph" title="Webgraph">webgraph</a> in memory.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  
</p><p>He joined the Personal Search Infrastructure team at <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> in 2014 where he worked on HappyHour, a processing and serving system for structured personal data. He then became a senior director of research engineering at <a href="/wiki/Google_Research" title="Google Research">Google Research</a> where he managed a team advancing the state of the art in information retrieval.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Marc then became a distinguished research scientist at <a href="/wiki/Google_Deepmind" class="mw-redirect" title="Google Deepmind">Google Deepmind</a> where he works on <a href="/wiki/Generative_artificial_intelligence" title="Generative artificial intelligence">generative artificial intelligence</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Honors_and_fellowships">Honors and fellowships</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Najork&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Honors and fellowships"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
