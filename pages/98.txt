<p><b>Filippo Menczer</b> (born 16 May 1965) is an American and Italian academic. He is a University Distinguished Professor and the Luddy Professor of <a href="/wiki/Informatics_(academic_field)" class="mw-redirect" title="Informatics (academic field)">Informatics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer Science">Computer Science</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Luddy_School_of_Informatics,_Computing,_and_Engineering" title="Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering">Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering</a> of <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University" title="Indiana University">Indiana University</a>. Menczer is the Director of the Observatory on Social Media,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> a research center where data scientists and journalists study the role of media and technology in society and build tools to analyze and counter disinformation and manipulation on social media. Menczer holds courtesy appointments in <a href="/wiki/Cognitive_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Cognitive Science">Cognitive Science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">Physics</a>, is a founding member and advisory council member of the IU Network Science Institute,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> a former director the Center for Complex Networks and Systems Research,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> a senior <a href="/wiki/Research_fellow" title="Research fellow">research fellow</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kinsey_Institute_for_Research_in_Sex,_Gender,_and_Reproduction" class="mw-redirect" title="Kinsey Institute for Research in Sex, Gender, and Reproduction">Kinsey Institute</a>, a fellow of the Center for Computer-Mediated Communication,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and a former <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">fellow</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Institute_for_Scientific_Interchange" title="Institute for Scientific Interchange">Institute for Scientific Interchange</a> in <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>, Italy. In 2020 he was <a href="/wiki/List_of_fellows_of_the_Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="List of fellows of the Association for Computing Machinery">named</a> an <a href="/wiki/ACM_Fellow" title="ACM Fellow">ACM Fellow</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Education,_career,_service"><span id="Education.2C_career.2C_service"></span>Education, career, service</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Filippo_Menczer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education, career, service"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Menczer holds a <a href="/wiki/Laurea" title="Laurea">Laurea</a> in physics from the <a href="/wiki/Sapienza_University_of_Rome" title="Sapienza University of Rome">Sapienza University of Rome</a> and a <a href="/wiki/PhD" class="mw-redirect" title="PhD">PhD</a> in computer science and cognitive science from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_San_Diego" title="University of California, San Diego">University of California, San Diego</a>. He used to be an assistant professor of <a href="/wiki/Management_science" title="Management science">management sciences</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Iowa" title="University of Iowa">University of Iowa</a>, and a fellow-at-large of the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe_Institute" title="Santa Fe Institute">Santa Fe Institute</a>. At <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_Bloomington" title="Indiana University Bloomington">Indiana University Bloomington</a> since 2003, he served as division chair in the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_School_of_Informatics" class="mw-redirect" title="Indiana University School of Informatics">Luddy School</a> in 2009â€“2011. Menczer has been the recipient of <a href="/wiki/Fulbright_Program" title="Fulbright Program">Fulbright</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rotary_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="Rotary Foundation">Rotary Foundation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> fellowships, and a <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation_CAREER_Award" title="National Science Foundation CAREER Award">CAREER Award</a> from the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a>. He holds editorial positions for the journals <i>Network Science</i>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/European_Physical_Journal" title="European Physical Journal">EPJ</a> Data Science</i>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/PeerJ" title="PeerJ">PeerJ</a> Computer Science</i>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Shorenstein_Center_on_Media,_Politics_and_Public_Policy#Combating_fake_news_&amp;_Harvard_Kennedy_School_Misinformation_Review" title="Shorenstein Center on Media, Politics and Public Policy">HKS Misinformation Review</a></i>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He has served as program or track chair for various conferences including <a href="/wiki/The_Web_Conference" title="The Web Conference">The Web Conference</a> and the <a href="/wiki/ACM_Conference_on_Hypertext_and_Social_Media" title="ACM Conference on Hypertext and Social Media">ACM Conference on Hypertext and Social Media</a>. He was general chair of the <i>ACM <a href="/wiki/Web_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Web Science">Web Science</a> 2014 Conference</i><sup id="cite&#95;ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and general co-chair of the <i><a href="/wiki/Netsci_Conference" title="Netsci Conference">NetSci</a> 2017 Conference</i>.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Research">Research</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Filippo_Menczer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Research"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Menczer's research focuses on Web science, social networks, social media, social computation, Web mining, data science, distributed and intelligent Web applications, and modeling of complex information networks. He introduced the idea of <a href="/wiki/Focused_crawler" title="Focused crawler">topical and adaptive Web crawlers</a>, a specialized and intelligent type of <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">Web crawler</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Menczer is also known for his work on <i>social <a href="/wiki/Phishing" title="Phishing">phishing</a>,</i><sup id="cite&#95;ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> a type of phishing attacks that leverage friendship information from social networks, yielding over 70% success rate in experiments (with <a href="/wiki/Markus_Jakobsson" title="Markus Jakobsson">Markus Jakobsson</a>); <a href="/wiki/Semantic_similarity" title="Semantic similarity">semantic similarity</a> measures for information and social networks;<sup id="cite&#95;ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> models of complex information and <a href="/wiki/Social_network" title="Social network">social networks</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Vespignani" title="Alessandro Vespignani">Alessandro Vespignani</a> and others);<sup id="cite&#95;ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Internet_censorship" title="Internet censorship">search engine censorship</a>;<sup id="cite&#95;ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Web_search_engine#Search_engine_bias" class="mw-redirect" title="Web search engine">search engine bias</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The group led by Menczer has analyzed and modeled how <a href="/wiki/Meme" title="Meme">memes</a>, information, and misinformation spread through <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> in domains such as the <a href="/wiki/Occupy_movement" title="Occupy movement">Occupy movement</a>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the Gezi Park protests,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and political elections.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Data and tools from Menczer's lab have aided in finding the roots of the <a href="/wiki/Pizzagate_conspiracy_theory" title="Pizzagate conspiracy theory">Pizzagate conspiracy theory</a><sup id="cite&#95;ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and the disinformation campaign targeting the <a href="/wiki/White_Helmets_(Syrian_Civil_War)" class="mw-redirect" title="White Helmets (Syrian Civil War)">White Helmets</a>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and in taking down voter-suppression bots on Twitter.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Menczer and coauthors have also found a link between online <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_misinformation" title="COVID-19 misinformation">COVID-19 misinformation</a> and <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_vaccine_misinformation_and_hesitancy" title="COVID-19 vaccine misinformation and hesitancy">vaccination hesitancy</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Analysis by Menczer's team demonstrated the <a href="/wiki/Echo_chamber_(media)" title="Echo chamber (media)">echo-chamber</a> structure of information-diffusion networks on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_elections" title="2010 United States elections">2010 United States elections</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The team found that conservatives almost exclusively retweeted other conservatives while liberals retweeted other liberals. Ten years later, this work received the Test of Time Award at the 15th International AAAI Conference on Web and Social Media (ICWSM).<sup id="cite&#95;ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> As these patterns of polarization and segregation persist,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Menczer's team has developed a model that shows how social influence and unfollowing accelerate the emergence of online echo chambers.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Menczer and colleagues have advanced the understanding of information virality, and in particular the prediction of what memes will go viral based on the structure of early diffusion networks<sup id="cite&#95;ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and how competition for finite attention helps explain virality patterns.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In a 2018 paper in <i>Nature Human Behaviour,</i> Menczer and coauthors used a model to show that when agents in a social networks share information under conditions of high information load and/or low attention, the correlation between quality and popularity of information in the system decreases.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> An erroneous analysis in the paper suggested that this effect alone would be sufficient to explain why fake news are as likely to go viral as legitimate news on Facebook. When the authors discovered the error, they retracted the paper.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Following influential publications on the detection of <a href="/wiki/Astroturfing" title="Astroturfing">astroturfing</a><sup id="cite&#95;ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>47<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>48<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Social_bot" title="Social bot">social bots</a></i>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>49<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>50<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Menczer and his team have studied the complex interplay between cognitive, social, and algorithmic factors that contribute to the vulnerability of social media platforms and people to manipulation,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>51<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>52<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>53<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>54<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and focused on developing tools to counter such abuse.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>55<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>56<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Their bot detection tool, Botometer, was used to assess the prevalence of social bots<sup id="cite&#95;ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>57<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>58<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and their sharing activity.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>59<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Their tool to visualize the spread of low-credibility content, Hoaxy,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>60<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>61<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>62<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>63<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> was used in conjunction with Botometer to reveal the key role played by social bots in spreading low-credibility content during the <a href="/wiki/2016_United_States_presidential_election" title="2016 United States presidential election">2016 United States presidential election</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>64<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>65<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>66<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>67<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>68<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Menczer's team also studied perceptions of partisan political bots, finding that Republican users are more likely to confuse conservative bots with humans, whereas Democratic users are more likely to confuse conservative human users with bots.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>69<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Using bot probes on Twitter, Menczer and coauthors demonstrated a conservative political bias on the platform.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>70<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>As social media have increased their countermeasures against malicious automated accounts, Menczer and coauthors have shown that coordinated campaigns by inauthentic accounts continue to threaten information integrity on social media, and developed a framework to detect these coordinated networks.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>71<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> They also demonstrated new forms of social media manipulation by which bad actors can grow influence networks<sup id="cite&#95;ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>72<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and hide high-volume of content with which they flood the network.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>73<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> 
</p><p>Menczer and colleagues have shown that political audience diversity can be used as an indicator of news source reliability in algorithmic ranking.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>74<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Textbook">Textbook</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Filippo_Menczer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Textbook"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>The textbook <i>A First Course in Network Science</i> by Menczer, Fortunato, and Davis was published by <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_University_Press" title="Cambridge University Press">Cambridge University Press</a> in 2020.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>75<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The textbook has been translated into Japanese, Chinese, and Korean.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Projects">Projects</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Filippo_Menczer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Projects"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<ul><li>Observatory on Social Media (OSoMe, pronounced <i>awesome</i>):<sup id="cite&#95;ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>76<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A research center aimed to study and visualize how information spreads online.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>77<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Includes data and tools to visualize <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> trends, diffusion networks, detect social bots, etc.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>78<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>79<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>
<ul><li>Botometer:<sup id="cite&#95;ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>80<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A machine learning tool to detect social bots on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>. Previously known as <i>BotOrNot</i>. Includes a public API, a social bot dataset repository, and the <i>BotAmp</i> tool<sup id="cite&#95;ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>81<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> to assess the role of automated accounts in boosting a given topic.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Hoaxy:<sup id="cite&#95;ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>82<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> An open-source search and network visualization tool to study the spread of narratives on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>. Includes a public API.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Fakey:<sup id="cite&#95;ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>83<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A mobile game for news literacy. Fakey mimics a social media news feed where you have to tell real news from fake ones.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Scholarometer:<sup id="cite&#95;ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>84<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A social tool and API to facilitate <a href="/wiki/Citation_analysis" title="Citation analysis">citation analysis</a> and help evaluate the <a href="/wiki/Citation_impact" title="Citation impact">impact</a> of an author's publications. By <a href="/wiki/Crowdsourcing" title="Crowdsourcing">crowdsourcing</a> discipline annotations, this <a href="/wiki/Browser_extension" title="Browser extension">browser extension</a> is able to provide a universal metric to compare impact across disciplines.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>85<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>86<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>87<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>88<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>
<ul><li>Kinsey Reporter:<sup id="cite&#95;ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>89<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A global mobile survey platform to share, explore, and visualize anonymous data about sex and sexual behaviors. Developed in collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/Kinsey_Institute_for_Research_in_Sex,_Gender,_and_Reproduction" class="mw-redirect" title="Kinsey Institute for Research in Sex, Gender, and Reproduction">Kinsey Institute</a>.
