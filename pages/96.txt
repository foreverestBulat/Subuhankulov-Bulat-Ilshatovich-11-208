</div><div class="mw-content-ltr mw-parser-output" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Former academic search engine</div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1316064257">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent;color:inherit}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:-3px}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}@media screen{html.skin-theme-clientpref-night .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data:not(.notheme)>div:not(.notheme)[style]{background:#1f1f23!important;color:#f8f9fa}}@media screen and (prefers-color-scheme:dark){html.skin-theme-clientpref-os .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data:not(.notheme)>div:not(.notheme)[style]{background:#1f1f23!important;color:#f8f9fa}}@media(min-width:640px){body.skin--responsive .mw-parser-output .infobox-table{display:table!important}body.skin--responsive .mw-parser-output .infobox-table>caption{display:table-caption!important}body.skin--responsive .mw-parser-output .infobox-table>tbody{display:table-row-group}body.skin--responsive .mw-parser-output .infobox-table th,body.skin--responsive .mw-parser-output .infobox-table td{padding-left:inherit;padding-right:inherit}}</style><table class="infobox vcard"><caption class="infobox-title fn org">Microsoft Academic Search</caption><tbody><tr><td colspan="2" class="infobox-image"><span class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Frameless"><a href="/wiki/File:Microsoft_academic_search_logo.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/e/e9/Microsoft_academic_search_logo.png" decoding="async" width="169" height="59" class="mw-file-element" data-file-width="169" data-file-height="59" /></a></span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><div style="display: inline-block; <p><b>Microsoft Academic Search</b> (<b>MAS</b>) was a research project and academic search engine retired in 2012. It relaunched in 2016 as <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Academic" title="Microsoft Academic">Microsoft Academic</a>, which in turn was shut down in 2022. The content of the latter was allegedly incorporated into <a href="/wiki/The_Lens" title="The Lens">The Lens</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Academic_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Microsoft launched a search tool called Windows Live Academic Search in 2006 to directly compete with Google Scholar.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was renamed <a href="/wiki/Live_Search_Academic" title="Live Search Academic">Live Search Academic</a> after its first year and then discontinued two years later.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-ReferenceA&#95;3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2009, Microsoft Research Asia Group launched a beta tool called Libra in 2009, which was for the purpose of algorithms research in object-level vertical search,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-msr&#95;news&#95;vertical&#95;search&#95;4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-msr_news_vertical_search-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Data_mining" title="Data mining">data mining</a>, <a href="/wiki/Entity_linking" title="Entity linking">entity linking</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Data_visualization" class="mw-redirect" title="Data visualization">data visualization</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-microsoft&#95;academic&#95;search&#95;project&#95;description&#95;5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-microsoft_academic_search_project_description-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Libra was redirected to the MAS service by 2011 and contained 27.2 million records for books, conference papers, and journals.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-ReferenceA&#95;3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Although largely functional, the service was not intended to be a production website and ceased to be developed, as was originally intended when the research goals of the project had been met.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-microsoft&#95;academic&#95;search&#95;help&#95;page;&#95;current&#95;6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-microsoft_academic_search_help_page;_current-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The service stopped being updated in 2012.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Microsoft&#95;Academic&#95;Search&#95;FAQ&#95;7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Microsoft_Academic_Search_FAQ-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-1404.7045&#95;8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1404.7045-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The fact that this decline was not reported on earlier indicated to the authors that the service was largely ignored by academics and bibliometricians alike.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-1404.7045&#95;8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1404.7045-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2014, Microsoft Research announced that <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Academic" title="Microsoft Academic">Microsoft Academic</a> was evolving from a research project to a production service, and would be integrating with Microsoft's flagship search engine, <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bing (search engine)">Bing</a>, and its intelligent personal assistant service, <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Cortana" class="mw-redirect" title="Microsoft Cortana">Cortana</a>. “By growing Microsoft Academic Search from a research effort to production,” [Microsoft Research's Kuansan] Wang says, “our goal is to make Bing-powered Cortana the best personal research assistant for our users".<sup id="cite&#95;ref-cortana&#95;research&#95;9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cortana_research-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
